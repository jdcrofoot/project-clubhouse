# Project Clubhouse Website — Strategy and Storytelling Evolution

## Context

Project Clubhouse has evolved significantly since the current website was first created.

The original concept centered on a fitness club with a social lounge and an integrated workout app.

The concept is now broader and clearer:

> **Project Clubhouse is a membership-based third place built around fitness, community, presence, and belonging.**

The performance floor remains essential. Fitness is the common language and the reason many members first walk through the door.

However, the larger value proposition is that Project Clubhouse becomes the place where teenagers, young adults, parents, athletes, students, mentors, and community members naturally choose to spend time.

Members may come to:

* Train
* Watch a game
* Meet friends
* Study
* Join a pickup basketball game
* Attend an event
* Grab an affordable protein shake
* Participate in a Link Up
* Connect with a team or Circle
* Simply hang out in a healthy, active environment

The website should now communicate this complete vision.

# The Core Positioning

The website should no longer present Project Clubhouse primarily as:

* A better gym
* A workout app
* A fitness center with extra amenities

Instead, it should present Project Clubhouse as:

> **The modern third place where people train, connect, and keep showing up.**

Supporting ideas:

> **Fitness is our common ground. Community is our advantage.**

> **Come for the workout. Stay for the community.**

> **Digital amplifies real life.**

> **Presence creates participation.**

> **Project Clubhouse does not replace real friendships. It creates more opportunities for them to happen.**

Do not use all of these lines at once. Selectively place them where they strengthen the story.

# Recommended Website Narrative

Reorganize the website into the following story.

## 1. Hero — The Big Idea

The hero must communicate the concept immediately.

Recommended headline direction:

# MORE THAN A GYM.

# THE PLACE TO SHOW UP.

Alternative:

# TRAIN. CONNECT. BELONG.

Supporting copy:

> A modern fitness and social club where members train, watch the game, study, meet friends, join activities, and become part of a real community.

Primary CTA:

**Explore the Club**

Secondary CTA:

**See How It Works**

Avoid leading with “Join Now” before visitors understand the concept.

The hero imagery should feature:

* The performance floor clearly visible
* The Clubhouse sportsbook lounge
* Diverse members
* A Pulse Board or digital leaderboard
* A sense that the entire building is active

The first impression should remain fitness-forward while clearly showing that the experience extends beyond the workout.

## 2. The Problem — Where Do People Go?

Create a concise section explaining the gap Project Clubhouse fills.

Suggested headline:

# THE PLACES PEOPLE USED TO GATHER ARE DISAPPEARING.

Supporting ideas:

* Traditional community centers feel outdated or have disappeared.
* Restaurants require ongoing spending and are not designed for teenagers to stay for hours.
* Libraries no longer function as the default social-study destination for many students.
* Most gyms are designed for people to work out and leave.
* Digital platforms connect people online but often leave them isolated in real life.

End with:

> Project Clubhouse creates a safe, active, affordable place where people can spend meaningful time together.

Keep this section optimistic. Do not make it sound like a societal crisis manifesto.

# Final Product Test

After updating the site, a new visitor should be able to answer these questions within approximately one minute:

1. What is Project Clubhouse?
2. Why is it different from a traditional gym?
3. Who is it for?
4. Why would someone stay longer than their workout?
5. How does PrimalTrack improve the physical club?
6. How do presence, Circles, Link Ups, and Pulse Boards build community?
7. Why would a parent pay for this?
8. Why would a teenager or young adult actually want to go?
9. How is the concept designed to remain affordable?
10. Why does the experience become more valuable as more people join?

The final website should leave visitors with one central feeling:

> **Project Clubhouse is where people go to get stronger, find their people, and keep showing up.**
