Project Clubhouse Website – Phase 5: Refine the Story, Not the Features
Context

The website has reached a strong point visually and structurally.

This phase is not a redesign.

It is a refinement.

The goal is to make the website feel less like a product demo and more like the beginning of a movement.

Everything should become simpler, more emotional, and more memorable.

Do not remove the existing design language.

Preserve:

Overall architecture
Color palette
Typography
Motion
Components
Existing pages
Existing responsive behavior

This phase should focus on improving storytelling, hierarchy, copywriting, and emotional impact.

Guiding Principle

Stop selling features.

Start selling transformation.

Every section should answer:

How does Project Clubhouse improve someone's life?

Not:

"What features do we have?"

Rewrite Every Section Around Outcomes

Instead of leading with product features, lead with emotional benefits.

Examples:

Instead of:

Club Live

Use:

Never wonder who's here again.

Instead of:

Link Ups

Use:

Make plans in seconds.

Instead of:

Circles

Use:

Find your people.

Instead of:

Progress Tracking

Use:

See how far you've come.

Instead of:

Fuel Station

Use:

Healthy shouldn't cost $12.

Instead of:

Performance Floor

Use:

Where beginners feel welcome and athletes feel challenged.

Instead of:

Sports Lounge

Use:

The place you'll still be sitting two hours after your workout.

Instead of:

Outdoor Patio

Use:

Some of the best conversations happen after the workout ends.

The emotional headline should always appear before the explanation.

Reduce Copy

The website currently explains too much.

Reduce written copy approximately 20–25%.

Replace paragraphs with:

stronger headlines
larger visuals
concise supporting text

Trust the visuals to tell more of the story.

Create more breathing room throughout the site.

Strengthen the Hero

Keep:

MORE THAN A GYM. THE PLACE TO SHOW UP.

Do not change this headline.

Improve the supporting copy.

The hero should immediately communicate:

A modern community built around fitness.

Not simply another gym.

Fitness is the invitation.

Belonging is the reason people stay.

Reframe the PrimalTrack Section

Do not introduce PrimalTrack as "our app."

Instead introduce it as:

The Club, In Your Pocket.

Lead with:

Then explain:

The emphasis should be community rather than workout logging.

Preserve Simplicity

Do NOT add more features.

Do NOT add more pages unless necessary.

Improve clarity instead of increasing complexity.

Final Website Test

After implementation, a first-time visitor should leave believing:

This is not another gym.
This is where people naturally spend time.
Fitness is only part of the experience.
The technology strengthens the real-world community.
Parents would trust it.
Teenagers would actually choose it.
The concept feels achievable rather than overly luxurious.

The website should create one lasting impression:

"I wish this existed when I was growing up—and I'd join today."

One More Thing...

I think you're exactly right to revisit the names.

And I don't think this is just a branding exercise.

It's a positioning exercise.

Six weeks ago, "Project Clubhouse" and "PrimalTrack" made sense because we thought we were building a fitness concept with a fitness app.

Now we're building something much broader:

a physical destination,
a digital community,
a network,
and eventually, I think, a brand that people identify with.

I actually think the company name, the club name, and the app name should be designed together as one cohesive system rather than independently. I have a lot of ideas there—including some directions that are much stronger than either current name—but I would treat that as its own strategic workshop. If we get those right, they'll carry this vision for decades.
