Update Vision Board Images

The vision boards have become one of the strongest parts of the project. I do not want to redesign them.

Instead, I want to improve the realism and inclusiveness of the imagery while preserving the exact visual style, layout, lighting, typography, and overall design language.

The existing aesthetic is our direction.

Black backgrounds
Industrial architecture
Warm lighting
Neon green accents
Athletic but approachable
Premium through design (not luxury)
Community-first

Please treat these changes as Version 1.1, not a redesign.

Overall Goal

When someone looks at these vision boards, I want them to think:

"I could actually see myself here."

Right now, many of the images unintentionally skew toward:

Mostly teenage boys
Mostly serious lifting
One demographic

Our club is much broader than that.

Diversity Improvements

Without changing the overall tone, increase representation throughout the vision boards.

Include more:

Females

Not as background characters.

Show them actively:

lifting
coaching
studying
hanging out
using the app
working at the Fuel Station
celebrating progress

They should feel equally represented.

Age Diversity

Represent multiple age groups naturally.

Examples:

13–15 year olds
High school athletes
College students
Young professionals
Parents
Adults training together

Avoid making everyone look like 22-year-old fitness models.

Different Fitness Levels

Show people who look like:

Beginners
Everyday gym members
Competitive athletes
Parents
Friends training together

This club is built for progress—not just elite performance.

Ethnic Diversity

Naturally represent a wide range of backgrounds.

Avoid making diversity feel forced.

The goal is authenticity.

Emotional Diversity

Not every image should show someone deadlifting.

Show moments like:

laughing with friends
celebrating a PR
helping someone learn an exercise
parents talking
teammates watching a game
someone studying
someone getting a protein shake
friends taking a selfie
someone encouraging another member
members watching the leaderboard together

Community should feel visible.

Most Important Change

Right now many images communicate:

"Fitness."

I want them to communicate:

"Belonging."

Fitness brings people in.

Community keeps them here.

Every image should reinforce that philosophy.

What NOT to Change

Do NOT change:

typography
color palette
industrial aesthetic
layout
vision board structure
lighting style
premium look
branding

These are already very strong.

Simply evolve the imagery to better reflect the community we are building.

Final Design Principle

When someone finishes looking at these vision boards, they should think:

"This isn't a gym for bodybuilders. This is a place where anyone who wants to improve can find their people."

That should be the feeling every image reinforces.
